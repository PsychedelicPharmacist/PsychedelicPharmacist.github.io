Wellness of the 'Well'

Mon, Nov 11, 2024 – Tue, Nov 11, 2025

← Back to Educational Events

Psychedelics could benefit society and humankind…

Beyond individual healing, psychedelics may offer profound benefits for collective wellness and social harmony. As we face unprecedented global challenges, these substances might help us reimagine our relationships with each other and our world.

The Social and Collective Dimensions of Wellness

True wellness extends beyond the individual. We are fundamentally social beings, and our health is deeply intertwined with the wellness of our communities, societies, and the collective human experience. When we speak of the "wellness of the well," we're considering how psychedelics might benefit not just individuals, but our entire social fabric.

Research is increasingly showing that psychedelic experiences can foster prosocial attitudes and behaviors that ripple outward, potentially transforming communities and societies.

Enhanced Empathy and Compassion

Multiple studies have demonstrated that psychedelic experiences can increase empathy, compassion, and prosocial behavior. For example:

  • Research with MDMA shows significant increases in empathic concern and perspective-taking
  • Psilocybin studies reveal increased emotional empathy that persists well beyond the acute experience
  • LSD has been shown to blur the distinction between self and other, potentially facilitating deeper connection

In a world increasingly divided by polarization and tribalism, substances that can help us feel genuine empathy for those unlike ourselves may have profound implications for social harmony and collective problem-solving.

Reducing Authoritarianism and Dogmatism

Interestingly, research has found correlations between psychedelic use and reduced authoritarian political views. A 2017 study published in the Journal of Psychopharmacology found that psychedelic experiences were associated with more liberal and libertarian views, reduced authoritarianism, and greater nature-relatedness.

Similarly, studies have found that psychedelic experiences can reduce cognitive rigidity and dogmatic thinking, potentially opening people to more flexible, nuanced understanding of complex issues. This could be particularly valuable in addressing polarized social and political discourse.

Community and Connection

Beyond individual shifts in perspective, psychedelics have historically played important roles in building community cohesion. From indigenous ceremonial use to the communal experiences of the 1960s counterculture, these substances have often been vehicles for shared experience and meaning-making.

In contemporary clinical settings, group psychedelic sessions are showing promise for building supportive community among participants, and integration circles provide spaces for collective processing and meaning-making.

Reduced Criminality and Antisocial Behavior

Studies have found associations between psychedelic use and reduced violent crime and recidivism. For example:

  • A 2017 study found that inmates who participated in ayahuasca ceremonies showed significant reductions in aggression and improved behavior
  • Research has found associations between psychedelic use and lower rates of intimate partner violence
  • Population studies suggest correlations between psychedelic use and reduced odds of criminal behavior

While correlation doesn't prove causation, these findings suggest potential pathways through which psychedelic experiences might reduce harmful behaviors in society.

Social Applications and Future Directions

As psychedelic research continues to expand, several promising directions for social applications are emerging:

  • Conflict Resolution: Could psychedelic-assisted therapy help address deep historical and social conflicts by fostering empathy, forgiveness, and reconciliation?
  • Leadership Development: Might carefully guided psychedelic experiences help develop more compassionate, creative, and inclusive leaders across sectors?
  • Environmental Action: As psychedelics reliably increase nature-connectedness, could they help catalyze the collective shift in consciousness needed to address environmental challenges?
  • Community Healing: Could psychedelic ceremonies adapted for contemporary contexts help rebuild fractured community bonds and foster collective resilience?

Ethical Considerations

While the potential social benefits of psychedelics are compelling, important ethical questions must be addressed:

  • How can we ensure that the benefits of psychedelic therapies are accessible across social and economic divides?
  • How do we respectfully incorporate indigenous wisdom while avoiding cultural appropriation?
  • What safeguards are needed to prevent manipulation of powerful psychedelic states for problematic social or political ends?
  • How might we balance individual sovereignty with collective responsibility in psychedelic policy?

These questions require ongoing dialogue across disciplines and communities as psychedelics continue their reemergence into mainstream awareness.

Conclusion: From "Me" to "We"

Perhaps one of the most profound aspects of many psychedelic experiences is the shift from an isolated sense of self to a deeply felt recognition of our fundamental interconnectedness—what philosopher Martin Buber might call a move from "I-It" to "I-Thou" relationships with others and the world.

As researcher Rosalind Watts has eloquently described, psychedelics often help people move from disconnection to connection—with themselves, others, and the broader web of life. In a world facing complex collective challenges, from climate change to social fragmentation, this shift from "me" to "we" consciousness may be exactly what's needed for true wellness of the well.
